DEVELOP THE WAY YOU THINK AS A DESIGNER

DECK OF CARDS AND A WEBSITE WITH REFERENCES FOR DESIGNERS

2020 / PRODUCT DESIGN

Role

UI designer, Developer

Duration

10 weeks, Winter 2020

Purpose

School Group Project

Develop the way you think as a designer

Develop the way you think as a designer was created by a group pf three designers with a strong belief in design as a problem-solving tool. As Master’s Designer students we create work we love and belief in. This project, called “Develop the way you think as a designer”, was born in our Communication Design subject at FCTUC (Faculty od Sciences and Technology, University of Coimbra).

Problem scope

The educational system does not differentiate all types of intelligence necessary for the practice of design, we will attack the problem in those who practice it.

User/customer

  • Learning design teams
  • Companies, studios, ateliers, coworking spaces

    • Solution

  • Editorial object: Letters Kit (set of printed physical letters + instructions/indications + support to store the kit)
  • Technological object: Website (HTML + CSS + Java Script + PHP)

    • Both supports complement each other in an informative and dynamic way. The recourse to the use of the same happens in the context of project development (initial phase of the project/brainstorming). The main objective is to stimulate the application of the different intelligences (PACES) in this procedure. Provide the necessary tools for a designer to be able to stimulate the different intelligences and apply them in the different phases of a design problem.

    PRODUCT

    Concept

    Having a wide variety of perspectives at the table throughout the design process allows us to design more inclusive experimences for a broader audience. The key for a good design is diversity. Nowadays, traditional design education models focus primarilt on formgiving and not enough attention is given to teaching students about how design functions in broder contexts, why it is culturally relevant, and how and why it now has the widespreas economic, political, environmental and technological impacts that it does. This project allows users to develop the way they thing as designers, providing a varied set of references (videos, podcasts, books, magazines, articles and webpabes) for each of the five itellegences explored, though the use of a deck of cards and a website.

    “An overview of Multiple Intellegence theories is procided to supposr a proposed framework for design education called the PACES Multiple Intelligences Model for Design (PACES = Pactical, Analytical, Creative, Emotional, and Social). PACES components can be effectively applied to supposr the needs of conteporary design educators and sutudents shared.” in a Multiple Intelligences Model for Design by Dennis M. Cheatham.

    Operating circuit

    1) The first contact with these resources is made through the website. Regarding editorial support, the deck is composed of different cards grouped together. These are identified by different colors that distinguish and stimulate the different types of intelligences in the design process. In this way, the designer has the possibility to exercise a single intelligence or work them randomly as a team. Thus, communication and knowledge sharing are encouraged, making the process more dynamic.

    2) The use of the deck of cards is complemented by navigation on the website. This allows designers to access various information, tools and references categorized by intelligence, enabling a better understanding of their application.

    RESEARCH

    Concepts studied

    Intelligences

    1. Set of all mental functions whose object is knowledge;
    2. Faculty of understanding;
    3. Conceptual and rational knowledge;
    4. Normal or above average mental development;
    5. Penetration of discernment;
    6. Person who has great intellectual gifts.

    Education

    1. Set of pedagogical norms aimed at the general development of the body and spirit;
    2. Knowledge and practice of uses considered socially correct;

    Design

    1. Discipline that aims to create objects, environments, graphic works, at the same time functional, aesthetic and in accordance with the imperatives of industrial production;
    2. Set of objects created according to these criteria;
    3. Appearance of a product created according to these criteria.

    Content references

    The content provided in the product was divided into five intelligences: practical, analitycal, creative, emotional and social. Different types of media were collected as content reference: books, magazines, articles, webpages, apps, podcasts, videos, newsletters and others. A total of 142 references were collected for content.

    After a selection process and an analysis of references, in which we normalize the references for each of the intelligences and filter by relevance. Thus, 125 references were included in the product.

    EDITORIAL OBJECT

    Brand identity

    Deck of cards

    10 cards per intelligence — total of 50 cards

    Each letter had a task/promp and a code for a reference to be consulted on the website. Through the reference code it was possible to access a reference that was related to the taks/promp and could help the designers to better understand the task or even complete it. In order to dynamize and boost the initial phase of the project/brainstorming.

    Brand identity

    TECHNOLOGIC OBJECT

    Definition

    The website is a platform with 125 references categorized by the five intelligences and media used. The platform also provides a contextualization of the project, the intelligences and the link to the editorial object is also made.

    Each reference is represented in a box, with a representative image, with the media to which it refers, the different intelligences that it allows to work with, the name of the media, the author and the reading time. This arrangement of the references allows for a better understanding and a greater interest in reading. It is also possible to filter the references through filters by intelligences.

    The implementation of the website was done with HTML, CSS, JavaScript (JSON and jQuery)

    User flow & sketches

    We explored a few user flows to determine what way would be the one to present all the information and have an navigation that was intuitive and dynamic with the use of the cards.

    Our primary concern was questioning which composition could present information and field inputs most clearly without being cluttered, yet visually engaging.
