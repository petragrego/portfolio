Cuidar de ti

CUIDAR DE TI was created by a group of three designers with a strong belief in design as a problem-solving tool. As Master’s Designer students we create work we love and belief in. This project, called “Cuitar de ti”, was born in our Service Design subject at FCTUC (Faculty od Sciences and Technology, University of Coimbra).

Problem scope

Health Professionals are constantly subject to high levels of stress in the work context. Burnout, a syndrome characterized by emotional exhaustion, depersonalization and low professional fulfillment, is one of the main results. Currently, we are witnessing its trivialization and stigmatization, which sensitized us to make this the focus of our project.

User/customer

Health professionals in the initial phase of rupture of the Burnout syndrome.

  • User A — Altruistic user who relativizes the stress arising from the syndrome.
  • User B — User who is proactive in seeking psychological support and is self-aware of the syndrome.

    • Solution

    Cuidar de ti is a service that aims to help healthcare professionals who are in burnout or have high levels of stress. The service consists of an app and two physical spaces. The app aims to support health professionals through contact with other people who are going through the same and also allow them to have moments of relaxation throughout their working day. The app allows healthcare professionals to access to physical spaces, namely the Relax Room and the Exercise Room. These have as main objective to give health professionals a place to relax or to exercise, in order to get away from the stress experienced in their work.

    RESEARCH

    Desk research

    Initially, a desk research was carried out in which several websites, videos and articles were consulted that addressed various health problems of health professionals in order to understand which problem we should address in this project.

    Desk research revealed that:
    1) There is an inherent need for excessive productivity;
    2) There is a stigma/taboo regarding Burnout;
    3) The main factors that lead a Health Professional to reach the maximum of exhaustion are inadequate ratios and the deontological duty to care and help;
    4) There is no concern, within the Medical Units, to break the tension and stress caused by work;

    Field research

    After the desk research, the field research took place. The field research was carried out through an interview with several health professionals or people related to them, and a form filled out by health professionals on the subject of burnout. These two activities made it possible to corroborate the information gathered from desk research.

    The interviews were carried out with 6 people: a nurse, a medical student, a nursing student, a nurse's daughter, a doctor and a senior nurse. In this interview, the participants were asked about the difficulties felt at work, the stigma about burnout, the impact that covid had on their lives, the pressure and excessive effort in the profession.

    The form was filled out by 12 health professionals with different professions and functions. This form questioned the experience of burnout in their work environment, and ways of relieving or helping to combat it.

    This field research allowed us to deepen our knowledge about burnout in healthcare professionals and to understand what would be the best possible service to help fight bournout.

    Value proposition canvas (VPC)

    Take care of yourself, that you take care of others!

    Do you feel tired? Do you live in intense restlessness? Our service is an opportunity for you to learn how to manage your emotions and conflicts in the work environment. We are concerned with centralizing useful information so that you can develop coping mechanisms.

    Those who take care, also need to take care of themselves!

    Our service offers you all the tools and methods you need to be at your best. It is essential to establish an emotional balance between the work and personal context. With us, you can find a quick and efficient solution.

    Match between customer profiles and VPC

    Desk research proved to be important in defining our problem scope and in creating the materials (Guide to Interviews and Survey Questions) that were later used in field research. The last one served as support for the developed JTBDs that helped in the construction of the VCPs.

    In this way, the interconnection of the data acquired by field research with the items of the VCPs was detailed through a table organized by the themes addressed. In order to make the reading clearer, the table has been divided between the items of each user and the items that both have in common. Code associated with each VCP item was also created.

    Service blueprint

    We have identified moments that have the greatest impact on mitigating our problem scope. Thus, the key moments identified are the use of the Relax Room, the scheduling of a psychology/psychiatry consultation and participation in a class in the Exercise Room. With this mapping, we intend to guarantee the symbiosis between the different moments, all the processes and functionalities that support them.

    SERVICE

    App

    Schedule an appoitment

    The app presents a feature of appointements, where the user is able to book an appoiment with the specifications wanted, se the availability and reschedule or cancel the appointment if needed.

    Mensage a friend/college

    The app allows the users to send mesages to theur friends or colleges, in order to, encourage and support one another.

    Share a testemony

    The feature of testemonials allows the users to share their testemony and to feel inspired by other testemonies.

    Check your calendar

    The app presents a calender so that users can check their work life and accomodate different classes or activities.

    Go to Relax Room

    The access to the Relax Room is done though the app. A QR Code is generated that works as a key to enter the room. The Relax Room feature also provides users with diferente types of breating and meditation exercises.

    Go to Exercise Room

    The access to the Exercise Room is done though the app. A QR Code is generated that works as a key to enter the room. The Exercise Room feature also provides users with the on going classes and the next classes. Users can also check the classes scheduled and book a class.

    Phisical spaces

    Relax Room

    The Relax Room provides users with an enviroment completly different than the hospital one. The confortable harmchairs, the plans and the headphones allow users to relax and take time for theirselves.

    Exercise Room

    The Exercise Room provides users with an enviroment of activity and abstraction from their work lives. The room is full of exercise equipment and numerous classes so that users can relax and exercise in their workplace, promoting physical exercise.

    VALIDATION

    Service viability test

  • Google Forms Service Feasibility Questionnaire, applied to the general community of healthcare professionals. Participants were 16 health professionals who responded to aspects of the service, such as time management.

  • Service Walkthrough with 4 participants. Creation of a hypothetical narrative that allowed the user to immerse himself in the physical experience and the technological artifact, which was demonstrated through a visual presentation. Through the Script, the narrator was responsible for reading the tasks of each Stage while the other member wrote down all the observations of the interaction.

  • Conduction of usability tests of the app.

  • Validation of the service blueprint, such as demo and focus on functionality.
